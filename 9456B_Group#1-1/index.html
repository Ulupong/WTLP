Religious Tolerance

Saint Aloysius Gonzaga Parish

The Saint Aloysius Gonzaga Parish is mainly the one who operates religious activities inside the campus of Saint Louis University. These religious activities is aiming to sustain the Christian and maturity growth of every students, whether their religions are different from one another. The SLU Parish Office is not requiring students or employees to be a Catholic to serve different activities.

Ofcourse, in a parish church they are offering liturgical services that will enhanced the capabilities of a person to become a better version of him/her. These services are like Sunday or weekday Masses, Baptisms, Confirmations, Confessions, Weddings, etc. Government officials or those people who are representatives of different religious or non-religious community will have the priviledge to join different programs that the Parish Church is conducting in a day-to-day living.

Aside from the SLU Parish Office is not requiring anyone to be a Catholic to work or serve in Parish Office. The gender is also not their priority either the person is a male or female, etc.

Religious Tolerance

The focus of the Parish Office is to make or to push every individual to become a better version of themselves. So that they can share it outside the campus in other people, and lead others, to transform everyone to become a better server of their church, either that church is a catholic or non-catholic church.
